## 1. POLICY OVERVIEW AND OBJECTIVES

### 1.1 Policy Statement

Global Bank Nigeria Limited is committed to preventing the use of its products and services for money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes. This Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) Compliance Policy establishes the framework for identifying, assessing, and mitigating money laundering and terrorist financing risks.

### 1.2 Policy Objectives

**Primary Objectives:**
- Detect and prevent money laundering activities
- Identify and report suspicious transactions
- Verify customer identity and assess risk
- Maintain compliance with all applicable AML/CFT laws and regulations
- Protect the bank's reputation and integrity
- Contribute to the global fight against financial crime

**Specific Objectives:**
- Implement robust customer due diligence procedures
- Establish effective transaction monitoring systems
- Maintain comprehensive record-keeping practices
- Provide regular training to staff on AML/CFT requirements
- Conduct periodic risk assessments and audits
- Foster a culture of compliance throughout the organization

### 1.3 Scope of Policy

This policy applies to:
- All departments and business units of Global Bank Nigeria Limited
- All employees, officers, and directors
- All banking products and services
- All customer relationships and transactions
- Third-party service providers and partners
- All subsidiaries and affiliated entities

## 2. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

### 2.1 Nigerian Laws and Regulations

**Primary Legislation:**
1. **Money Laundering (Prohibition) Act 2011 (as amended)**
   - Prohibits money laundering activities
   - Establishes penalties and sanctions
   - Defines money laundering offenses

2. **Terrorism (Prevention) Act 2011 (as amended)**
   - Prohibits terrorist financing
   - Establishes reporting requirements
   - Defines terrorism offenses

3. **Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (Establishment) Act 2004**
   - Establishes EFCC mandate and powers
   - Defines EFCC functions and operations

4. **Banks and Other Financial Institutions Act (BOFIA) 2020**
   - Establishes banking regulatory framework
   - Defines CBN supervisory powers
   - Sets prudential requirements

5. **Nigeria Financial Intelligence Unit (NFIU) Act 2018**
   - Establishes NFIU mandate and functions
   - Defines reporting requirements
   - Establishes information sharing mechanisms

### 2.2 Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) Regulations

**Key CBN Regulations:**
1. **CBN AML/CFT Regulations 2022**
   - Comprehensive AML/CFT framework
   - Customer due diligence requirements
   - Transaction monitoring and reporting

2. **CBN KYC Regulations 2021**
   - Customer identification procedures
   - Risk-based approach to KYC
   - Documentation requirements

3. **CBN Electronic Banking Guidelines**
   - Digital banking AML/CFT requirements
   - Online transaction monitoring
   - Electronic customer verification

4. **CBN Cryptocurrency Guidelines 2023**
   - Virtual asset service provider requirements
   - Cryptocurrency transaction monitoring
   - Digital asset AML/CFT measures

### 2.3 International Standards

**FATF Recommendations:**
- Financial Action Task Force (FATF) 40 Recommendations
- FATF Risk-Based Approach Guidance
- FATF Virtual Assets Guidance

**Other International Standards:**
- Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS) Principles
- Wolfsberg Group AML Principles
- Egmont Group FIU Standards
- United Nations Security Council Resolutions

## 4. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD) PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Customer Identification

**Individual Customers:**

**Primary Identification Documents:**
- National Identity Card (NIN)
- International Passport
- Driver's License
- Voter's Card

**Secondary Documents:**
- Utility Bill (Not older than 3 months)
- Bank Statement (Not older than 3 months)
- Tax Clearance Certificate
- Employment Letter

**BVN Verification:**
- Bank Verification Number (BVN) mandatory for all customers
- BVN verification through NIBSS platform
- Cross-reference with other banks

**Biometric Verification:**
- Facial recognition verification
- Fingerprint verification
- Digital signature verification

**Corporate Customers:**

**Required Documents:**
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Memorandum and Articles of Association
- CAC Forms (CAC 1.1, CAC 2.1, CAC 7)
- Board Resolution to open account
- Tax Identification Number (TIN)
- Utility Bill of registered office
- Identity documents of directors and signatories
- Company profile and business activities

**Additional Requirements:**
- Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO) identification
- Ownership structure documentation
- Business registration verification
- Industry and sector analysis

### 4.2 Customer Acceptance Policy

**Customer Risk Categories:**

**Low Risk Customers:**
- Nigerian citizens with valid BVN
- Salary earners with verifiable employment
- SMEs with established operations
- Government agencies and parastatals
- Regulated financial institutions

**Medium Risk Customers:**
- Non-resident individuals
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Foreign corporate entities
- Non-bank financial institutions
- Non-profit organizations

**High Risk Customers:**
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Cash-based businesses
- High-value transactions
- Jurisdictions with weak AML/CFT regimes
- Virtual asset service providers
- Casinos and gambling establishments

### 4.3 Customer Risk Assessment

**Risk Assessment Factors:**

**Customer Factors:**
- Customer type and occupation
- Geographic location
- Business activities
- Transaction patterns
- Source of funds/wealth
- Relationship complexity

**Product/Service Factors:**
- Type of product/service
- Transaction volume and frequency
- Payment methods used
- Geographic scope
- Cash handling requirements
- Online/electronic access

**Geographic Factors:**
- Country of residence
- Country of business operations
- Jurisdiction risk rating
- Sanctions status
- AML/CFT regime quality

## 6. TRANSACTION MONITORING AND REPORTING

### 6.1 Transaction Monitoring System

**Automated Monitoring:**
- Real-time transaction monitoring system
- Rule-based alert generation
- Pattern recognition algorithms
- Behavioral analysis
- Cross-border transaction monitoring

**Monitoring Parameters:**
- Transaction amounts and frequency
- Geographic locations
- Counterparty relationships
- Transaction types and methods
- Cash transaction thresholds
- Unusual patterns and behaviors

### 6.2 Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)

**Reporting Thresholds:**
- **Cash Transactions:** Report single or related cash transactions exceeding ₦5,000,000 (individuals) or ₦10,000,000 (corporates)
- **Electronic Transfers:** Report unusual or suspicious electronic transfers regardless of amount
- **Cross-Border Transactions:** Report all international transfers exceeding ₦10,000
- **Suspicious Activity:** Report any transaction suspected to involve money laundering or terrorist financing

**STR Submission Process:**
- Complete STR form within 24 hours of suspicion
- Submit to Nigeria Financial Intelligence Unit (NFIU)
- Copy to Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN)
- Maintain confidentiality of STR
- Do not inform customer of STR submission

**STR Content Requirements:**
- Customer details and account information
- Transaction details and dates
- Reasons for suspicion
- Supporting documentation
- Relationship to customer's profile
- Any additional relevant information

### 6.3 Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)

**CTR Requirements:**
- Report all cash transactions exceeding ₦5,000,000 (individuals) or ₦10,000,000 (corporates)
- Submit CTR within 15 days of transaction
- Submit to NFIU and CBN
- Maintain records for 7 years

**CTR Content:**
- Customer identification
- Transaction amount and date
- Currency and denomination
- Source of funds
- Purpose of transaction
- Recipient information (if applicable)

### 6.4 Terrorist Financing Reporting

**Terrorist Financing Indicators:**
- Transactions involving sanctioned individuals or entities
- Transactions to/from high-risk jurisdictions
- Unusual charitable or donation activities
- Complex or opaque transaction structures
- Use of shell companies or trusts
- Rapid movement of funds across borders

**Reporting Process:**
- Immediate report to NFIU
- Notification to CBN
- Notification to law enforcement agencies
- Account freezing where appropriate
- Full documentation and evidence preservation

### 7.1 Customer Records

**Customer Information Records:**
- Identification documents (copies)
- Customer risk assessment
- KYC forms and applications
- BVN verification records
- Biometric verification data
- Correspondence with customers

**Retention Period:**
- Customer identification records: 7 years after account closure
- Transaction records: 7 years from transaction date

- AML/CFT training records: 7 years from training date

### 7.2 Transaction Records

**Required Information:**
- Transaction date and time
- Transaction amount and currency
- Originating and receiving parties
- Transaction type and method
- Purpose of transaction
- Supporting documentation

### 7.3 Reporting Records

**STR/CTR Records:**
- Submitted STRs and CTRs
- Acknowledgment receipts
- Investigation reports
- Follow-up actions taken
- Communication with regulatory authorities

## 12. ENFORCEMENT AND SANCTIONS

### 12.1 Internal Sanctions

**Staff Non-Compliance:**
- Warning letters
- Suspension
- Disciplinary action
- Termination of employment
- Referral to law enforcement

### 12.2 Regulatory Sanctions

**CBN Sanctions:**
- Fines and penalties
- Operating restrictions
- License suspension
- License revocation
- Management changes

### 12.3 Criminal Sanctions

**Money Laundering Offenses:**
- Imprisonment up to 14 years
- Fines up to the equivalent of the laundered funds
- Forfeiture of assets
- Debarment from banking operations

**Terrorist Financing Offenses:**
- Imprisonment up to 20 years
- Fines up to ₦50,000,000
- Forfeiture of assets
- Debarment from banking operations
